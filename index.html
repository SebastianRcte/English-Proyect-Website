Welcome to San Nicolas De Los Arroyos

General Information

Welcome to San Nicolas De Los Arroyos, a vibrant city located in Buenos Aires Province, Argentina. Known for its rich history and beautiful landscapes, our city offers a unique blend of culture and modernity.

  • Founded: 1748
  • Population: Approximately 145,000 (as of 2010)
  • Area: 680 square kilometers
  • Climate: Humid subtropical

History and Origins

San Nicolas De Los Arroyos has a rich history dating back to the 18th century. Originally founded as a small settlement, the city has evolved into an important industrial and cultural center in Argentina.

Key Historical Events:

  • 1748: Foundation of San Nicolas De Los Arroyos
  • 1852: The Agreement of San Nicolas was signed here
  • 1983: Apparitions of the Virgin Mary reported, leading to the construction of the Sanctuary

Tourist Attractions

Sanctuary of Our Lady of the Rosary

A beautiful sanctuary built in honor of the reported apparitions of the Virgin Mary. It attracts thousands of pilgrims each year.

Rafael de Aguiar Municipal Theatre

A historic theatre built in 1908, known for its beautiful architecture and cultural performances.

Parana River Coastline

Enjoy beautiful views and recreational activities along the scenic Parana River coastline.

Traditions and Legends

San Nicolas De Los Arroyos is rich in traditions and fascinating legends that have been passed down through generations.

Annual Festivals:

  • Festival of the Virgin of the Rosary - September 25th
  • San Nicolas City Foundation Day - April 14th

Local Legend: The Apparitions of the Virgin

The city is famous for the reported apparitions of the Virgin Mary to a local woman in the 1980s, which led to the construction of the Sanctuary and made San Nicolas a site of pilgrimage.

Local Cuisine

The culinary scene in San Nicolas De Los Arroyos reflects the rich Argentine traditions with a local twist.

Asado

Traditional Argentine barbecue, featuring various cuts of meat grilled to perfection.

Locro

A hearty stew made with corn, beans, meat, and vegetables, typically served on patriotic holidays.
